About Me

Jesus Pineda Ramirez is a scientist and public health advocate based in California. He previously worked at Stanford Medicine and the University of California, San Francisco. His research spans viral dynamics, immunity, epidemiology and community health.

As an immigrant rights and healthcare advocate, Jesus partners with undocumented and immigrant families to improve access to care and build community resilience. In the clinic he profiles host responses to infection, and in the lab he develops serology tools that measure antibodies against multiple targets. His community‑focused projects track viruses in wastewater, study long COVID and assess programs like Park Rx that connect families with nature. Jesus aims to bridge bench science, public health and immigrant justice.
